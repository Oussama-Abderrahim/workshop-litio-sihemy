Sihem Amer-Yahia
Directrice de Recherche CNRS (Grade Exceptionnel) · Directrice adjointe, LIG Grenoble

Sihem Amer-Yahia est Directrice de Recherche CNRS (Médaille d'Argent 2020) et Directrice adjointe du Laboratoire d'Informatique de Grenoble (LIG). Ses travaux portent sur l'analyse exploratoire de données, la montée en compétences algorithmique et l'équité dans les systèmes pilotés par les données. Avant de rejoindre le CNRS, elle a été Principal Scientist au QCRI (Qatar), Senior Scientist chez Yahoo! Research, et Member of Technical Staff chez AT&T Labs. En 2024, elle a reçu simultanément le Prix IEEE TCDE, le Prix ACM SIGMOD Contributions et le Prix VLDB Women in Database Research — une triple distinction exceptionnelle qui témoigne de son impact international.

Exposé invité
Policy Reusability for Multi-Objective Sequential Decision Making
Dr. Sihem Amer-Yahia · CNRS / LIG Grenoble

Abstract — Multi-objective sequential decision making (MO-SDM) is central to many real-world tasks, where an agent must make a sequence of decisions that balance multiple, often conflicting objectives. Multi-objective reinforcement learning (MORL) is a common approach to solving MO-SDM problems, but typically requires training from scratch under known objective configurations. In this talk, we propose a novel zero-shot paradigm: reusing a set of existing or pre-trained single-objective (SO) policies. We discuss cases where policy reusability comes with theoretical guarantees and cases where we use large language model (LLM)-driven orchestration. We present empirical results in Education showing that our method achieves competitive Pareto quality and per-objective performance while reducing computational cost by 3× compared to MORL methods. We further discuss open questions on how LLM context richness and reflective foresight enable zero-shot decision quality.
